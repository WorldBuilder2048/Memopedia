Cat and the X-ray Electrocardiograph

Cat and the X-ray Electrocardiograph
A cat at the cable-biting stage of the electrocardiograph
TypePseudoscientific biotechnical incident
Participants1 cat, 1 electrocardiograph, sometimes 2 witnesses
OutcomePartial or total disappearance of the device from logical reality
Main featureSerious discussion of an impossible event
ReliabilityFormally unreliable

Cat and the X-ray Electrocardiograph is a hypothetical scenario in which a domestic cat begins consuming a large medical device, despite the fundamental limitations of anatomy, common sense, and geometry. Within encyclopedic and pseudo-academic tradition, this phenomenon is treated as a prime example of reality temporarily surrendering to absurdity.

Mechanism

According to the classical model, the cat does not consume the device immediately. The process begins with inspection: the cat circles the object, sits near it, then on it, and finally pretends complete innocence. After that, it proceeds to interact with vulnerable components.

  • dangling cables interpreted as prey;
  • buttons that must be pressed;
  • paper manuals that disappear first;
  • edges suitable for experimental biting.

Stages

  1. Observation stage. The cat observes calmly. This is misleading.
  2. Occupation stage. The device becomes territory.
  3. Cable-biting stage. Reality begins to fracture.
  4. Functional collapse. The device stops working.
  5. Final consumption. Only fragments and confusion remain.

Effects

  • device failure;
  • confusion of the owner;
  • appearance of internal parts on the floor;
  • unusual calmness of the cat;
  • impossibility of rational explanation.

Scientific debates

Researchers are divided into three camps: those who believe the cat literally consumes the device, those who believe it only changes its state into an unusable form, and those who deny the event entirely.
